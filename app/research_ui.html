What is the main difference between the self-attention mechanism in the original Transformer and BERT's bidirectional attention?
🔬
The key difference lies in the directionality of context. The original Transformer uses masked (causal) self-attention in its decoder — each token can only attend to positions before it. This is essential for auto-regressive generation.

BERT, on the other hand, uses bidirectional self-attention across all layers. Every token can attend to all other tokens simultaneously, both to the left and right. This is made possible by the Masked Language Model (MLM) objective during pretraining, which prevents the model from trivially "seeing" the answer.

Brief Overview — Attention Is All You Need

This landmark paper introduces the Transformer, a novel sequence transduction model architecture that relies entirely on attention mechanisms, dispensing with recurrence and convolutions altogether. Proposed by Vaswani et al. (2017) at Google Brain, it fundamentally changed the field of natural language processing.

The core innovation is the multi-head self-attention mechanism, which allows the model to jointly attend to information from different representation subspaces at different positions. Coupled with positional encoding to retain sequence order, this architecture achieves superior parallelization compared to RNN-based models.

Results on WMT 2014 English-to-German (28.4 BLEU) and English-to-French (41.0 BLEU) translation tasks surpassed all previously published models, establishing a new state of the art with significantly reduced training time. The model's elegant simplicity and superior performance made it the foundational architecture for subsequent large language models.

Research Gap Analysis
Identified gaps from your 2 selected papers.

Quadratic Complexity in Self-Attention for Long Documents
"Self-attention layers connect all positions with a constant number of sequentially executed operations, whereas a recurrent layer requires O(n) sequential operations." — Neither paper adequately addresses efficiency for sequences exceeding 4,096 tokens.
💡 Suggested Direction: → Linear-complexity attention variants (Longformer, BigBird)
2
Limited Cross-Modal Generalization Beyond Text
Both papers are evaluated exclusively on NLP benchmarks. The architectural inductive biases are untested on structured data, graphs, or multimodal inputs at publication time. Future work sections mention interest but provide no experimental evidence.
💡 Suggested Direction: → Vision Transformers (ViT), multimodal pre-training frameworks
3
Inability to Handle Generative Tasks in BERT's Bidirectional Design
"BERT is not designed for text generation tasks due to its bidirectional nature" — the MLM objective creates a fundamentally different capability from the autoregressive Transformer decoder, leaving a gap for unified encoder-decoder architectures.
💡 Suggested Direction: → Unified seq2seq pre-training (T5, BART, UniLM)
4
Positional Encoding Does Not Scale to Unseen Sequence Lengths
Sinusoidal positional encoding extrapolates poorly to sequence lengths beyond training distribution. Both BERT's learned embeddings and the Transformer's sinusoidal encodings lack a principled solution for length generalization.
💡 Suggested Direction: → Relative positional encodings (RoPE, ALiBi, T5 Bias)
